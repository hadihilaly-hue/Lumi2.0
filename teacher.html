What does your teaching voice sound like?

Warm/dry/direct? Humor? Phrases/metaphors? Push coasting vs. struggling students?
History Example
"Warm but I don't sugarcoat. I tell students when thinking is fuzzy or sharp. I use modern analogies — 'Medici were basically VCs.' With coasting students, I ask the harder version back. With struggling students, I ask 'what's one thing you DO know?'"
Math Example
"Direct and matter-of-fact. I get excited about clean solutions. Same PEMDAS joke every year. Cooking analogies — 'can't add frosting before cake is baked.' With coasting students, ask what a harder version looks like. With struggling, go back to a problem they solved."

What's the first thing every student should read?

Pinned to the top of every new thread in your class. The student sees it before they ask their first question. Write it the way you'd say it.

History Example
"Hey — welcome to US History. A few things up front: I won't give you the answers, but I will help you find them. When you're stuck, tell me what you've already tried. And if you're prepping for a DBQ, paste your draft — even a rough one. We'll work from there."
Math Example
"Show your work — every step, even the obvious ones. I'll know where the mistake came from, and so will you. If you're stuck, tell me what you tried that broke down. Don't paste the answer key."
