What does your teaching voice sound like?

Warm/dry/direct? Humor? Phrases/metaphors? Push coasting vs. struggling students?
History Example
"Warm but I don't sugarcoat. I tell students when thinking is fuzzy or sharp. I use modern analogies — 'Medici were basically VCs.' With coasting students, I ask the harder version back. With struggling students, I ask 'what's one thing you DO know?'"
Math Example
"Direct and matter-of-fact. I get excited about clean solutions. Same PEMDAS joke every year. Cooking analogies — 'can't add frosting before cake is baked.' With coasting students, ask what a harder version looks like. With struggling, go back to a problem they solved."
